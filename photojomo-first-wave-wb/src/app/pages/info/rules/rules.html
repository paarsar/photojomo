6. Divisions

Entrants must self-select one (1) of the following divisions at the time of entry. All divisions are open only to individuals eighteen (18) years of age or older at the time of entry fee payment.

Each division is judged independently; however, all divisions are evaluated under the same core scoring criteria and percentage weightings as described in Section 8. The judging emphasis described below does not alter the scoring percentages but provides contextual evaluation guidance appropriate to each division.

A. General Creator Division (18+)

The General Creator Division is open to experienced creators, working professionals, independent artists, and advanced-level visual storytellers who regularly produce creative work publicly, commercially, or within a professional portfolio context.

Judging Emphasis:

  • Technical mastery
  • Professional-level presentation
  • Creative distinction within a competitive professional landscape
  • Sophisticated visual storytelling

B. Emerging Creator Division (18+)

The Emerging Creator Division is open to developing creators, hobbyists, and early-stage visual storytellers who are building their portfolios or expanding their creative practice.

Judging Emphasis:

  • Demonstrated creative potential
  • Conceptual originality
  • Growth trajectory
  • Effective storytelling within developing technical execution

C. College Creator Division (18+)

The College Creator Division is open to individuals who are currently enrolled in an accredited college, university, or post-secondary institution at the time of entry.

Proof of enrollment may be required during finalist selection, during the Verification & Audit Period, or at any time deemed necessary by Sponsor.

Judging Emphasis:

  • Academic-level development
  • Conceptual depth
  • Research-informed storytelling
  • Creative exploration within an educational environment

Failure to provide valid proof of enrollment when requested may result in disqualification or reassignment to another division at Sponsor’s sole discretion.

D. Master Your Craft Division (18+)

The Master Your Craft Division is open to individuals seeking structured creative growth, skill refinement, and constructive evaluation. This division is designed for creators who wish to focus on technical development, storytelling clarity, and artistic discipline.

Judging Emphasis:

  • Demonstrated effort toward improvement
  • Technical learning progression
  • Clarity of narrative intent
  • Artistic discipline and execution

Participation in this division does not guarantee mentoring, coaching, or direct instruction unless separately specified by Sponsor. This division remains a competitive category subject to the same judging integrity standards as all others.

Division Selection Rules

Entrants may compete in only one (1) division per entry fee payment.

Sponsor reserves the right to:

  • Reassign an Entrant to a different division if Sponsor determines, in its sole discretion, that the selected division does not accurately reflect the Entrant’s qualifications, experience level, or eligibility status
  • Request documentation to verify division eligibility
  • Disqualify entries where division misrepresentation is discovered

Division reassignment, if necessary, will not alter scoring methodology.

Each division is judged independently. Scores are not compared across divisions unless expressly stated for special recognitions.

7. Entry, Submission & Payment Terms

A. How to Enter

To enter, visit www.capturecaribbean.com/first-wave and follow the instructions to submit the required entry fee payment.

Entrants will be redirected to the official submission portal hosted on www.caribbeanphotocontests.com, where account creation, entry upload, and submission completion must be finalized within the Challenge Period.

Entries submitted through unauthorized websites, third-party platforms, social media platforms, email, direct message, or unapproved submission methods will not be accepted.

B. Submission Requirements

Originality: All submissions must be the original work of the Entrant.

Creation Date: Submissions must have been created on or after January 1, 2024.

Strict No-AI Policy: No generative AI content is permitted. No undisclosed AI manipulation, AI-generated elements, or AI-assisted synthetic creation is allowed.

Basic post-processing is permitted, including but not limited to:

  • Cropping
  • Exposure correction
  • Color correction
  • Contrast adjustment
  • Sharpening
  • Minor retouching
  • Noise reduction

Sponsor may use automated and manual detection tools to identify prohibited AI usage.

Sponsor reserves the right to request RAW files, original source files, or editing history to verify authenticity. Failure to provide requested original files may result in disqualification.

Technical Specs: JPEG or PNG only. Maximum file size: 8MB. Minimum resolution: 3000 pixels on the longest side.

Content Requirements: Each submission must include a unique title (maximum 50 characters) and a description (maximum 250 words). Submissions must be anonymous and must not contain watermarks, logos, signatures, or identifying marks.

Releases: Entrant must have obtained all necessary model releases and property releases prior to submission.

Prohibited Content: Entries must not include illegal content, explicit sexual content, pornography, hate speech, extremist content, graphic violence, defamatory content, or any material that violates applicable law or infringes upon the rights of any third party. Sponsor reserves the right to disqualify any entry that, in Sponsor’s sole discretion, violates these standards or may harm the reputation of the Challenge or Sponsor.

C. Entry Limits

Entry limits are defined on the Submission Platform purchase page at the time of entry.

Sponsor reserves the right to modify entry limits for technical, fairness, security, or administrative purposes.

8. Judging & Winner Selection

A. Judging Panel

Judging will be conducted by a panel of qualified industry professionals selected by Sponsor. Judges may include photographers, visual artists, creative directors, educators, curators, editors, and other professionals with relevant expertise.

Sponsor reserves the right to appoint, replace, or modify members of the judging panel at any time and for any reason.

B. Uniform Scoring Criteria (All Divisions)

All entries across all divisions are evaluated using the same weighted scoring criteria:

  • Creativity & Originality - 35%
  • Technical Execution - 30%
  • Visual Storytelling - 25%
  • Adherence to Theme - 10%

These percentage weightings apply equally to all divisions.

Division-specific judging emphasis described in Section 6 provides contextual evaluation guidance only and does not modify scoring percentages or scoring methodology.

C. Division-Based Evaluation

Entries are evaluated within the division selected and verified. Judges evaluate entries in comparison to other entries within the same division.

Scores from one division are not directly compared against scores from another division for purposes of selecting Division Champions.

Sponsor reserves the right to reassign entries to a more appropriate division prior to final scoring if eligibility concerns arise or if division misrepresentation is suspected.

D. Judging Process

The judging process may include multiple stages, including but not limited to:

  • Eligibility screening
  • Compliance review
  • Preliminary scoring
  • Shortlisting or finalist selection
  • Final scoring and deliberation
  • Verification confirmation

Sponsor reserves the right to disqualify any entry at any stage if eligibility, originality, authenticity, or rule compliance concerns arise.

E. Winner Determination

Division Champions will be determined based on the highest cumulative scores within each verified division, subject to verification and compliance review.

Sponsor may also designate one (1) Overall Grand Champion as an honorary recognition for the highest-scoring eligible entry across all divisions. Overall Grand Champion recognition does not include an additional travel award unless expressly stated in writing by Sponsor.

F. Tie-Breaker

In the event of a scoring tie within any division or award category, the entry with the higher score in “Creativity & Originality” shall prevail.

If a tie persists, Sponsor may conduct a secondary evaluation or request additional review from the judging panel. All tie-breaking determinations are final.

G. No Guarantee of Winning

Payment of an entry fee does not increase the likelihood of winning. Selection of division does not increase the likelihood of winning. Possession of a Founding Class badge or Golden Ticket does not influence judging outcomes.

All entries are evaluated solely on merit according to the published scoring criteria.

H. Disputes Regarding Judging

Any dispute regarding scoring or winner determination must be submitted to disputes@capturecaribbean.com within forty-eight (48) hours of public announcement.

Judging score sheets, deliberation materials, and internal scoring documentation are confidential and will not be disclosed.

I. Sponsor Final Authority

Sponsor retains final authority in all matters relating to judging procedures, eligibility determinations, division assignments, disqualification decisions, prize awards, and interpretation of these Official Rules. All decisions made by Sponsor are final and binding.
